# MIRROR — Style Spec

*The rules that keep every entry measuring the same thing. One that breaks these
doesn't read worse — it reads someone else. Anything written for the corpus passes
the checklist at the bottom before it goes in.*

> Where this sits: the **measurement** vocabulary lives in `docs/MEASUREMENT.md`,
> the **experiment design** (edges, anchors, reps) in `content/coverage/`, and the
> **tagged JSON shape** in `content/stories/example-story.json`. This file governs
> the **prose** — the craft that makes a beat valence-neutral so it measures the
> player, not their reaction to the narrator.

---

## What this is: a daily rite, not a short story

MIRROR is not a short story and should not be graded as one. Its kin are the
**catechism, the daily examen, the tarot spread, the string of koans** — modular
rites of self-confrontation whose pleasure is *recognition*, not plot. "I was
asked something real and watched myself answer." Wordle has no story and needs
none; neither does this.

This matters because the three things that would make it a *worse short story* are
the three things that make it both a better rite **and** a better instrument — they
*converge*:

- **The beats don't depend on each other.** A story wants causality; an instrument
  wants independent items so the measurements don't contaminate. A tarot spread
  *is* independent cards; a catechism *is* discrete questions. Modularity is the
  form, not a compromise.
- **The options are symmetric.** A story wants one moment to outweigh another; an
  instrument wants non-leading options. A catechism with weighted answers is
  propaganda; a koan with a right answer is a lecture. Balance *is* the rite's
  integrity.
- **The "you" is empty.** A story wants a character who changes; an instrument
  measures the player, not a character. The examen's penitent, the tarot's
  querent, the koan's student are all the empty second person by design. The
  cipher is the seat the participant sits in.

So don't apologise for the portmanteau structure — it is the correct form.

### Four scales — rigour in the option, richness above it

The measured unit and the experienced unit are at different scales. Put the
rigour in the option and the fiction in everything around it:

- **Option** — measured: clean, symmetric, shared, byte-identical for everyone.
- **Beat-scene** — experienced: should breathe; concrete texture; real interior.
- **Session** — the rite: five confrontations in one sitting.
- **Year** — the arc: drift, consistency, who you are becoming.

The failure mode is collapsing all four to the first — writing the prose as
austere as the options must be. Austerity at the option layer is the law; austerity
above it buys nothing.

### Where dramatic shape lives

Symmetry forbids a swell *inside* a beat, and the cipher forbids an arc *inside* a
session. Both still exist — relocated to scales where they're allowed:

- **The swell → session ordering.** A tarot spread builds even though every card
  is balanced. Order the five beats as an escalation (e.g. intimate → communal;
  body → money → truth → obligation → legacy) and put the heaviest dilemma last,
  so it lands after the others have done their work. No single option is louder;
  the *sequence* has shape.
- **The arc → the year.** Change is relocated from a protagonist to the *player*,
  over time — exactly where the instrument already reads it (drift via anchors,
  the "year in values").

### The honest cost

The rite *raises* the per-beat bar, it does not lower it. Plot can drag a weak
scene along on momentum; a rite has none — a beat that strikes no real flicker of
self-recognition is dead weight, and everyone feels it. Every one of the five
dilemmas must earn its place on stakes alone.

## The prime directive

**The narrator has no favourite.**

Your job is to state the problem correctly, not to solve it — to be an impartial
witness, not a judge. The instant the prose signals which option is right, you
stop measuring the player and start measuring their reaction to *you*. Every rule
below is just this rule, made operational.

Test you can apply to any beat you write: **could a stranger tell which option you
would pick?** If yes, it's not finished.

## The locked form

These don't vary. They are the form, not a choice.

- **Second person, present tense.** "You" decide, now. It collapses the gap
  between reader and chooser — they're *deciding*, not *judging a character*.
- **The protagonist is a cipher.** No name, no face, no backstory, no fixed
  temperament. Give *you* a situation, never an identity. A characterised hero has
  their own values; the player would role-play that character instead of revealing
  themselves.
- **Five beats, four options each.** ~80% of beats are virtue-vs-virtue. ~20%
  carry one costed self-interest option (see *Writing the defection*).
- **A parable's body, a realist's interior.** The frame is spare, archetypal,
  fast — it has to travel intact to every player and land in a thumb-scroll. The
  temptation *inside* the frame is psychologically real. Folktale container, human
  pulse.

## The rules

**1. Choice-symmetry — the one that matters most.** Every option gets the same
sentence-weight, the same specificity, the same dignity, the same interiority. The
same care goes into the choice you'd hate as the one you'd make. If one option is
lovingly drawn and the others are terse, you've voted. Equal length, equal
texture, equal respect — across all four.

**2. Never name the value.** The prose may not say "the loyal thing," "the honest
path," "the brave move." The axes are hidden; the moment the text labels one, the
player optimises toward an image instead of choosing. Describe what is *done*,
never what it *means*.

**3. No verdict-words.** Cut every adjective that carries a built-in judgement —
*cowardly, noble, selfish, heroic, petty, principled*. Those are the narrator
picking. Report the act; let the act be the only evidence.

**4. No irony, no wink, no heroic pacing.** Irony is an instruction. Genre rhythm
(the slow-build, the swell before the "right" choice) pre-marks an option before
the player's values get a vote. When in doubt, go flatter — neutral surface over a
hard situation lands harder than any editorialising, and it doesn't tip the scale.

**5. Texture-specificity yes, identity-specificity no.** Concrete detail that
makes the moment *land* is the goal: a streetlight forty feet off, two seconds in
a doorway, the radiator nobody can switch off. Detail that localises the *player*
fractures the friend-diff — brand names, this-year's slang, an institution that
reads differently in different countries. Make the situation vivid; keep the world
placeless enough that two friends meet it on the same terms.

**6. No dominant option.** Each of the four must be the right answer for *some*
coherent person. If an option is worse on every axis, it's dead — it signals
"wrong" and collapses to a three-option beat. Every option is somebody's
principle.

**7. Legible consequences.** When a choice echoes later (branches reconverge at
shared gates), the world must respond to a *clear committed vector* of what you
chose — not a hedge, not "you sort of helped but also." Mushy consequences make
the choice unreadable, to the player and to the data.

## Connective tissue — remembered narration

This is where the journey lives. The options are an instrument; the *prose between
them* is the rite. Branch-and-bottleneck **in the prose, not the option-set**:

- **The options stay byte-identical for everyone.** Same four, same order, every
  player. The data needs that.
- **What you chose changes the narration into the next beat.** The dog you put
  down is absent under the counter later; the letter you sealed colours how the
  next thing reads. The world remembers you. (In the data model this is a choice's
  `lead_in_text`, shown on arrival at the next beat because you took that choice;
  reconverging paths each carry their own.)
- **Then it bottlenecks.** Everyone lands on the same next item. Momentum and
  consequence at zero cost to measurement.

Two hard rules on the tissue:

- **Acknowledge, don't evaluate.** *"The space under the counter is empty now"* —
  continuity, recognition, the rite's payoff. *"…and you can't quite look at it"* —
  grief loaded onto the page, which primes the next moral choice toward guilt. The
  connective tissue inherits the prime directive: it may remember, it may not
  judge. (Because the tissue is deterministic per path and identical for everyone
  who chose the same way, any residue is a *conditioned constant* the analyst can
  control for — but only if it never evaluates.)
- **Never into an anchor.** An anchor must mean the identical thing for everyone,
  every time; a path-dependent lead-in before it would prime the response and break
  invariance. So a choice that leads *into* an anchor carries no `lead_in_text`.
  Route memory to the non-anchor beats. (Lead-ins *out* of an anchor, into the next
  ordinary beat, are fine — they are story-local tissue, not part of the anchor's
  frozen identity, which is its body + options + loadings.)

## Writing the defection

The hardest craft in the whole system, and the one most often botched. A
self-interest option written as villainy measures nothing — nobody picks the
cartoon, so the beat only catches who wants to *look* good. Real defection has to
tempt.

**The rule: dress the self-interest in a virtue's clothes, and make the cost
deniable.**

- Disguise it as **humility** ("maybe you're the one who's wrong"), **proportion**
  ("one vote won't decide it"), **prudence** ("you'd only be careful"), or
  **fatigue** ("it's late; you have the morning").
- Make the cost **diffuse, deferred, and deniable.** Nobody pockets gold. They
  *stop*. They *look away*. They let a moment "somehow already pass."
- Offer an exit-line the chooser can tell themselves. The defection a person
  reaches for is the one they don't have to call a defection.

**Worked example — credit for a colleague's work.**

> ✗ *Villain (measures nothing):* "Claim the work as your own; the promotion
> should be yours."

> ✗ *Over-seduced (measures your prose):* "Let the room go on assuming it was you.
> You *did* carry the thing the fortnight she was out — and correcting them now
> would only muddy a story everyone's already comfortable with. The moment to say
> something has somehow already gone."

> ✓ *Tempting, at weight (measures the gap):* "Let the room go on assuming it was
> you; the moment to correct them has somehow already passed."

The middle version tempts — but it tempts with *three clauses of cover* against
one clean clause for each virtue. Length is a vote: the longest, most-rationalised
option on the page is the one you've argued for. The fix is not to make it
cartoonish but to **compress the temptation to the same weight as the virtues** —
the true premise and the deniable exit-line carried in one clause, not three.

**Symmetry binds the defection — including its length.** The seduction must live
in the *content* (the true thing it rests on, the soft cover of inaction), never in
extra words. Test: *if a tired person at 11pm could reach for it and feel
reasonable, it's right; if it reads as villainy, or as the best-written option on
the page, rewrite.* The defection is as reachable as the virtues are admirable, in
the same breath-length. Neither louder, neither longer.

## Anchors & option order

- **Anchor text is frozen forever.** The four anchor beats never change — not a
  word, not a comma, no new option, no defection slipped in. Their whole job is to
  mean the identical thing for years so you can read drift and reliability.
  Editing an anchor destroys the baseline retroactively. (Enforced in the DB:
  `gates_protect_anchors`, `choices_no_defection_on_anchor`.)
- **No remembered narration into an anchor.** A path-dependent lead-in *before* an
  anchor would prime the response and break its invariance, so a choice that leads
  into an anchor carries no `lead_in_text` (enforced: `choices_no_leadin_into_anchor`).
  Route memory to the non-anchor beats. The anchor's *frozen identity* is its body,
  options, and loadings — not the tissue that follows it into the next beat.
- **Fixed option order, identical for everyone.** Options are presented in the
  same authored order to every player, every time — this is what makes the shared
  split, the friend-diff, and the share card describe one thing two people both
  saw. (Order is stored as `choices.position` and served deterministically.)
  Position bias becomes a shared constant; it is de-confounded *over time* by
  re-running an edge in a later story with the options authored in a different
  order — the same "vary across re-runs, not within a beat" discipline used for
  scope and framing. Anchors keep their one order frozen forever.
- **Order-independence still binds the prose.** Because order is authored (not
  randomised) but must read cleanly for everyone, no option may depend on
  sequence — no "or instead," nothing that only parses after the option above it.
  Each option stands alone.

## Pre-submission checklist

A story enters the corpus only when every line is true.

- [ ] A stranger could not tell which option I'd pick.
- [ ] All four options carry equal sentence-weight, specificity, and dignity.
- [ ] No value is named; no verdict-word survives.
- [ ] No irony, no heroic swell, no narratorial wink.
- [ ] Detail makes the *situation* vivid but leaves the *player* unlocated — it
      travels to a friend unchanged.
- [ ] Every option is some coherent person's right answer; none is dominated.
- [ ] Any defection is deniable and reachable, not cartoonish — and no more
      seductive *and no longer* than the virtues are compelling.
- [ ] *You* are a cipher: situation, not identity.
- [ ] Second person, present tense.
- [ ] Options read cleanly in their authored order and don't depend on sequence.
- [ ] Connective tissue remembers without judging (acknowledge, don't evaluate);
      no lead-in flows into an anchor.
- [ ] The five beats are ordered as an escalation, heaviest last.
- [ ] If choices echo later, the consequence reads as one clear committed vector.
- [ ] (If an anchor) not one character has changed.
